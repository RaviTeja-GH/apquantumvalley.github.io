Cybersecurity

Protecting systems, networks and data from digital attacks

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, servers, mobile devices, networks and data from unauthorized access, cyber attacks and damage. It ensures confidentiality, integrity and availability of information.

Common Types of Cyber Attacks

  • Phishing: Fake emails or links used to steal passwords or bank details.
  • Malware & Ransomware: Harmful software that damages systems or locks files for money.
  • Password Brute Force: Hackers try many passwords until they guess correctly.
  • Data Breach: Stealing sensitive company or user information.
  • Man-in-the-Middle Attack: Intercepting communication between two users.
  • Denial of Service (DDoS): Flooding servers to make websites crash.

Security Protection Methods

  • Strong passwords and multi-factor authentication (OTP verification)
  • Firewalls and antivirus software
  • Data encryption
  • Regular software updates and patches
  • Secure Wi-Fi networks
  • Backup and recovery systems

Why Cybersecurity is Important

  • Protects personal information
  • Prevents banking and financial fraud
  • Secures company networks and databases
  • Protects government and national infrastructure
  • Maintains privacy in digital communication

Cybersecurity Career Roles

  • Security Analyst
  • Ethical Hacker (Penetration Tester)
  • Security Engineer
  • Incident Responder
  • Cloud Security Engineer
  • Digital Forensics Investigator
